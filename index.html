Welcome to Material Failure Analysis, where we systematically examine physical and material-related causes of hard disk failures to identify root causes, improve reliability, and prevent recurrence.

About Us

The Material Failure Analysis Section (FAD3) is an integral part of the Failure Analysis Department (FAD) which is committed to identifying, diagnosing, and resolving HDD failures and defects with precision and efficiency.


Through rigorous investigation, advanced analytical techniques, and collaboration across teams, we strive to uncover root causes of failures, improve product reliability, and drive continuous innovation.

Our Services

Dis-Assy Inspection

This process involves visual inspection and disassembling key components such as Head Disk Assembly and Head Stack Assembly.

Optical Analysis

Sample Preparation and standard optical Inspection of heads, GMA checking and QST.

Atomic Force Microscopy

Confirmation of contamination and scratch appearance through topographical Data and Measurement of defects (e.g. depth, width, height).
Identication of the Yaw Angle and checking the corresponding radius and error address.

Optical Surface Analysis

Detection and classification of contaminants/defects such as particles, scratches, pits, and stains on transparent, semi-transparent, or opaque substrates and films.

Scanning Electron Microscopy

Ramp sampling analysis that uses focused beam of electrons to scan the surface of a Ramp sample, producing highly detailed images of its structure such as the backside lift tab and chamfer area.

Field Emission Microscopy

Contact point analysis of lube stains, scratches, abrasions and contaminations on the MR and ABS using electron microscope that uses a strong electric field to extract electrons from a sharp metallic tip, forming an image based on variations in electron emission.
